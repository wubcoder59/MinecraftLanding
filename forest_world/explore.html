Our Wilderness Trails

Explore our carefully maintained trail network, each offering unique ecosystems and experiences. From accessible boardwalks to challenging backcountry routes, there's a perfect path for every visitor.

Ancient Forest Loop

Distance 3.2 miles
Difficulty Moderate
Elevation Gain 450 ft
Estimated Time 2-3 hours

Journey through one of the oldest forest stands in the region, where towering oak, maple, and hemlock trees create a cathedral-like canopy overhead. This loop trail features several sections of old-growth forest that have remained undisturbed for centuries.

Notable features include the 300-year-old "Guardian Oak," natural spring seeps, and a diverse understory of ferns and woodland wildflowers. Spring brings an impressive display of trillium and spring beauties carpeting the forest floor.

This trail passes through sensitive habitat. Please stay on marked paths to protect rare plants and prevent soil erosion.

Crystal Creek Boardwalk

Distance 1.5 miles
Difficulty Easy
Elevation Gain Minimal
Estimated Time 1 hour

This fully accessible boardwalk trail winds alongside Crystal Creek, providing intimate views of this pristine waterway and the surrounding wetland ecosystem. The elevated wooden path protects the sensitive riparian habitat while allowing visitors to experience the rich biodiversity of this transitional zone.

Interpretive signs highlight the ecological importance of wetlands, the water cycle, and the many species that depend on this habitat. Viewing platforms extend over the water at several points, providing excellent opportunities for wildlife observation and photography.

This boardwalk is wheelchair accessible and suitable for all ages and abilities. Benches are provided every quarter mile.

Ridge Overlook Trail

Distance 4.8 miles
Difficulty Challenging
Elevation Gain 1,200 ft
Estimated Time 4-5 hours

For those seeking a more strenuous adventure, the Ridge Overlook Trail climbs through diverse forest zones to reach a spectacular viewpoint atop the eastern ridge. The trail begins in lowland hardwood forest, transitions through mixed pine-hardwood slopes, and emerges onto rocky outcrops with panoramic views of the entire wilderness area.

The demanding ascent rewards hikers with changing ecosystems and increasingly dramatic vistas. At the summit, Eagle's Point provides a breathtaking 180-degree view of the valley, distant mountains, and the meandering Crystal Creek watershed. Wildlife sightings often include soaring hawks and eagles riding thermal updrafts along the ridge.

This challenging trail requires proper footwear, ample water, and weather preparedness. Not recommended for inexperienced hikers or children under 12.

Wilderness Loop Trail

Distance 7.5 miles
Difficulty Moderate-Difficult
Elevation Gain 850 ft
Estimated Time 5-6 hours

The signature trail of Wilderness Haven offers an immersive full-day experience through nearly every ecosystem in the preserve. This well-marked loop traverses hardwood forests, pine stands, creek crossings, small meadows, and an impressive rock formation known as Cathedral Rocks.

Hikers will experience the full biodiversity of the region, with opportunities to observe wildlife throughout the varied habitats. The trail includes several rest areas and viewpoints, including Crystal Falls, a 25-foot waterfall that is most impressive after spring rains or during the fall color season.

Pack a lunch and plenty of water for this all-day hike. Trail maps are essential as cell service is limited in remote sections.

Woodland Observation Blind

This carefully designed hide allows visitors to observe forest wildlife without disturbing their natural behavior. Comfortable seating and viewing slots provide excellent visibility while keeping humans concealed.

Best time: Dawn or dusk for maximum activity

Common sightings include white-tailed deer, wild turkey, and various woodland birds

Hawk Ridge Overlook

Located on the eastern ridge, this viewpoint offers exceptional bird watching opportunities, especially during migration seasons when hawks, eagles, and other raptors ride thermal currents along the ridge.

Best time: Fall migration (September-October)

Bring binoculars! On peak days, observers may spot hundreds of raptors passing overhead

Wetland Platform

This elevated platform overlooks a beaver-created wetland that serves as a hub for aquatic wildlife. The shallow waters and diverse vegetation attract a wide variety of species.

Best time: Early morning or evening hours

Look for beaver lodges, great blue herons, various waterfowl, and occasional river otters

Leave No Trace

Plan Your Visit

Check trail conditions before starting your hike. Inform someone of your planned route and expected return time. Carry essentials including water, food, and appropriate clothing.

Wildlife Safety
